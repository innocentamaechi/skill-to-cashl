Sequence rules
WhatsApp Follow-Up Sequence

7 Days.
From Stranger to Buyer.

Every message in this sequence is written to do one job at a time — deliver value, build trust, create desire, handle objections, and close. In that order. Never skip a step.

Welcome
Day 0 — PDF Delivery
Send immediately on opt-in
Strategy
This is the first message they receive. It needs to do three things: deliver what was promised, set the expectation for what comes next, and create anticipation. Do not pitch anything here. Do not even hint at a paid product. Just be a generous host.
Message 1 — Welcome + PDF link Send immediately
Hey [Name] 👋 Welcome. The PDF is right here 👇 📄 [INSERT PDF LINK] This is the full V.I.R.A.L System breakdown — including the Bon Bread case study with actual numbers and the 50 AI prompts. Read it when you have 20 quiet minutes. Not while you're distracted. I'll be sending you a few messages over the next week with stuff that builds on it — practical, no fluff. If at any point you want off the list, just reply "stop" and I'll remove you immediately. Talk soon.
~120 words · Tone: warm, direct
Key note: The "reply 'stop'" line is not optional — it builds massive trust with cold audiences and keeps your WhatsApp number from being flagged as spam. People who know they can leave feel safer staying.

Engage

Send 48 hours after opt-in
Strategy

If they read this and think "this is literally me" — the sale is already psychologically made. You just need to give them a way to solve it (Days 4–6).

The most common objections in a Nigerian online audience: "I don't have time", "I'm not tech-savvy enough", "I've tried things before and they didn't work", "₦2,000 is still money." Address each one directly, then re-open the door.

Sequence Rules

Follow these without exception. Breaking any of them will reduce your conversion rate — some significantly.

One message per day. Never two.
Except on Day 4 and Day 5 where you send a follow-up. Every extra message you send outside that drops your response rate by 15–20%.
Always use their first name.
WhatsApp feels personal. Every message should feel like it was written for one person. "[Name]" is not optional — it's the difference between broadcast and conversation.
Never pitch before Day 4.
Days 0–3 are the foundation. If you pitch before it's set, you lose trust and the close. The sequence works precisely because the pitch is earned, not sprung.
Reply to everyone who responds.
People who reply to your broadcasts are giving you a gift. They self-identify as hot leads. Respond personally within 2 hours. These are your easiest closes.
Never beg. Never guilt-trip.
The sequence stays confident throughout. "No pressure" is in the copy for a reason — it's disarming, not weak. Desperation kills conversion faster than any other single factor.
Send in the morning. Always.
Between 7am–10am WAT. Nigerian WhatsApp users check their phones first thing. Messages sent after 3pm compete with work, traffic, and decision fatigue. Morning wins.
Track your reply rate daily.
If below 5% by Day 3, your audience is cold or the messages aren't landing. Test a different angle on Day 2 before you scale. Reply rate predicts sales 3 days early.
After Day 7, move them to a nurture list.
Anyone who hasn't bought after 7 days goes onto a lower-frequency list. Send them one message per week max with free value. They're not dead — they just need more time.
